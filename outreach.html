Outreach

Robotics Engineering

In 2010, Sehome High School began offering to VEX Robotics teams the ability to work during the Industrial Education class. These VEX teams were given a small room to work, building the robots in teams of two. Due to the success of our pilot teams (who won the Amaze Award), 2 additional teams were added in 2011. In 2012, we were granted access to the new Robotics Room and thus more space to work. In 2013, we added an FTC team into the mix; consisting of 4 advanced students.

By pointing out the benefits of robotics and STEM education to the Bellingham school district, we aided in the creation of a “Robotics Engineering” class, available in 2013-14. Students no longer have to work under the shadow of another class, but are granted full shop access for the now 6 VEX teams. We also added another FTC team as part of this class, both of which now have full use of the Robotics Room.

Lego Clubs

Kulshan Middle School of the Bellingham School District, expressed interest in forming a FLL team in 2011. We jumped on the opportunity to help, and have been mentoring the group and teaching the core principles to them since. In 2014, they are planning to register and compete with full FLL team status.

Public Events

Every year, the robot and team can be found at a few events in and around the Bellingham community.

Since 2010, we have been presenters at Linuxfest Northwest, a festival to promote and teach about various developing technologies. In the past, we have worked with the Bellingham Artificial Intelligence Society (B.A.I.R.S.) to provide a spectacular display for all to see. In 2013, we were granted the use of a full presentation room by ourselves, open to the public all day. This allows members of the community to come in and see what we and FIRST represent. When we were at Linuxfest this year, someone came up to us to ask questions about the mechanical workings of the robot, and video taped the interviews with the team members. This video became popular on the internet, and even got featured on the front page of slashdot.org. The video can be viewed here.

Starting in 2012, we began participating in Bellingham’s Annual Ski to Sea Parade. This event is incredibly popular in Bellingham, and is featured on TV as well. When we walk down the streets with Queequeg dancing and jumping, and the robot revved up to full, people see what FIRST is all about. This has brought the Bellingham community closer to the team, and is an excellent team bonding experience.
